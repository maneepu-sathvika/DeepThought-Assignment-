Proposal: 1000 ICP-Qualified Pharmaceutical Companies in One Month

PART-B  Submission – Business Analytics Internship



**Objective**



The objective is to build a verified list of 1000 pharmaceutical companies in India that match the defined ICP criteria within 30 days.



The final dataset should be clean, structured, and suitable for CRM and business development use. The focus is on accuracy, relevance, and verification rather than volume.



**Key Constraints**



• Only relevant and high-quality companies should be included

• Every company must be validated using reliable sources

• Expected success rate is around 25–35 percent

• Initial universe required: 3500–4000 companies to achieve 1000 valid outputs



**Overall Approach**



The process is divided into four stages:



• Building an initial universe of companies

• Filtering irrelevant or low-quality entries

• Verifying companies based on ICP criteria

• Final cleaning and structuring of the dataset



1\. **Building the Initial List (Week 1)**



The first step is to collect a broad set of pharmaceutical companies from reliable sources.



Government and Regulatory Sources



• Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) company registry

• CDSCO licensed drug manufacturer database

• State drug control department records



Industry Directories



• Pharmexcil exporter directory

• Indian Pharmaceutical Alliance member list

• CPhI India exhibitor lists

• BioAsia participant lists



Financial and Company Databases



• Tofler company database

• ZaubaCorp filings database

• Crunchbase pharma and biotech filters



Trade and Export Data



• Pharmaceutical export and import shipment records

• GST exporter databases



Industry Events



• CPhI India exhibitor lists

• Medicall Expo participant companies

• PharmaTech Expo directories



Output of Week 1



• Around 3500 to 4000 companies collected

• Basic details such as name, website, location, and segment captured

• Duplicate entries removed where possible



2\. **Filtering and Shortlisting (Week 2)**



This stage removes irrelevant or non-ICP companies.



Exclusion Criteria



• Companies that only trade or distribute products

• Hospitals or healthcare providers misclassified as pharma

• Inactive or dormant companies

• Companies with no verifiable online presence



Data Standardization



Each company is structured into:



• Company name

• Website

• Location

• Business segment (API, formulation, biotech, CDMO)

• Financial information (if available)

• Registration details (CIN or GST)



Output of Week 2



• Reduced dataset of approximately 1500 to 1600 companies

• Only semi-qualified companies retained



3\. **Verification and Shortlisting (Week 3)**



This stage ensures companies truly match ICP requirements.



Verification Checks



• Active and functional company website

• Evidence of manufacturing or pharmaceutical operations

• Export or production activity

• Financial scale or credibility indicators



Cross Verification Sources



• Company websites

• MCA filings

• Tofler financial data

• Export and import records



Handling Borderline Cases



• Manually reviewed one by one

• Either included with justification or removed



Output of Week 3



• Around 1500 companies reduced to 1000 to 1100 verified companies



4\. **Final Cleanup (Week 4)**



The final stage ensures the dataset is usable and structured.



Final Steps



• Remove duplicate entries

• Validate all company information

• Standardize formatting

• Ensure consistency across dataset



Final Output



• 1000 verified pharmaceutical companies in India

• Each company includes key structured information

• Ready for CRM use and outreach activities



Expected Outcome



• Initial universe: 3500 to 4000 companies

• After filtering: 1500 to 1600 companies

• Final verified dataset: 1000 companies



**Summary**



This process ensures a structured and verification-heavy approach to building a high-quality ICP dataset. Every stage focuses on reducing noise and improving accuracy so that the final 1000 companies are genuinely relevant and usable for business applications.
